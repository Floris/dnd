# Floryn Pocket Handbook
## Small lookup handbook for play

This document is built like a mini player handbook for **Floryn**.
Use it as a lookup reference during play.

For each entry, you can quickly see:
- **Type** = skill, spell, feature, attack, or utility
- **Cost** = what it takes to use
- **What it does** = the actual effect
- **Why you use it** = the practical reason to pick it

---

# Quick Reference

## Core numbers
- **Level:** 6 Ranger (Hunter)
- **AC:** 17
- **HP:** 52
- **Initiative:** +7
- **Speed:** 40 ft.
- **Passive Perception:** 16
- **Spell Save DC:** 14
- **Spell Attack Bonus:** +6
- **Spell Slots:** 4 level 1, 2 level 2
- **Free Hunter's Mark casts:** 2 per Long Rest

## Main combat identity
- Strong at **ranged combat**
- Best when fighting from **good position or stealth**
- Wants to pressure **one important target**
- Gets strong value from **Hunter's Mark**, **Extra Attack**, and **Colossus Slayer**

## Your usual combat turn
1. **Bonus Action:** cast **Hunter's Mark** on an important target
2. **Action:** take the **Attack** action with your **Longbow**
3. Because of **Extra Attack**, you make **2 attacks**
4. If the target is already wounded, **Colossus Slayer** can add extra damage once per turn

## Concentration reminder
You can only concentrate on **one** of these at a time:
- **Hunter's Mark**
- **Fog Cloud**
- **Pass without Trace**
- **Spike Growth**

If you start one of these, the other concentration spell ends.

# A–Z Lookup

## Acrobatics
**Type:** Skill

**Cost:** No action by itself; used when the DM calls for a check

**What it does:**
- Helps with balance, agile movement, and slipping through tricky spaces
- Your bonus is **+4**

**Why you use it:**
- Crossing narrow ledges
- Keeping your footing
- Doing agile movement where raw Strength is not the answer

---

## Alert
**Type:** Feat

**Cost:** Passive

**What it does:**
- Gives you proficiency in Initiative
- Helps explain why your Initiative is **+7**
- Lets you **swap initiative with an ally** if that is tactically better

**Why you use it:**
- You act early in combat more often
- You can let a frontliner or caster go first if the party needs that more

---

## Archery
**Type:** Fighting Style

**Cost:** Passive

**What it does:**
- Gives **+2 to attack rolls with ranged weapons**
- This is already included in your **Longbow +9** attack bonus

**Why you use it:**
- Makes your ranged attacks very reliable
- Helps you land both attacks from Extra Attack more often

---

## Athletics
**Type:** Skill

**Cost:** No action by itself; used when the DM calls for a check

**What it does:**
- Covers climbing, jumping, grappling, pushing, and pulling
- Your bonus is **-1**

**Why you use it:**
- Only when a Strength-based physical task comes up
- This is not one of your strong options

---

## Colossus Slayer
**Type:** Hunter feature

**Cost:** Passive; triggers **once per turn**

**What it does:**
- When you hit a creature that is **already wounded**, you can deal **+1d8 extra damage**
- This can happen **once per turn**

**Why you use it:**
- It rewards focusing targets that have already taken damage
- It works very well with **Extra Attack**, because two attacks make it easier to land at least one hit

**Best reminder:**
- Look for a target that has already been hurt before you shoot

---

## Cure Wounds
**Type:** Spell (Level 1)

**Cost:** **Action**, **1 spell slot**, **Touch**, no Concentration

**What it does:**
- Heals a creature you touch
- A basic emergency healing spell

**Why you use it:**
- Stabilizing the situation when someone is low
- Helping an ally get back on their feet if healing is urgently needed

**Best reminder:**
- This is useful, but it costs your action, so it competes with attacking

---

## Extra Attack
**Type:** Ranger feature

**Cost:** Passive; applies when you take the **Attack** action

**What it does:**
- When you take the **Attack** action, you attack **twice** instead of once

**Why you use it:**
- It is the core of your combat damage
- It works especially well with **Longbow** and **Hunter's Mark**

**Best reminder:**
- If you are making a normal weapon turn, your action is usually **Attack = 2 attacks**

---

## Favored Enemy
**Type:** Ranger feature

**Cost:** Passive

**What it does:**
- Keeps **Hunter's Mark** always prepared
- Gives you **2 free casts of Hunter's Mark per Long Rest**

**Why you use it:**
- Lets you use your signature combat spell efficiently
- Saves spell slots over the course of the day

---

## Fog Cloud
**Type:** Spell (Level 1)

**Cost:** **Action**, **1 spell slot**, **120 ft.**, **Concentration**

**What it does:**
- Creates a cloud that blocks vision in an area
- Makes sight-based fighting and targeting much harder

**Why you use it:**
- Breaking line of sight
- Covering an escape
- Creating chaos when enemies rely on vision

**Watch out:**
- It can also block your own longbow plan
- Casting this ends **Hunter's Mark**, **Pass without Trace**, or **Spike Growth** if you were concentrating on one of them

---

## Goodberry
**Type:** Spell (Level 1)

**Cost:** **Action**, **1 spell slot**, **Self**, no Concentration

**What it does:**
- Creates magical berries
- Each berry can restore a small amount of health
- They also provide a day's nourishment

**Why you use it:**
- Efficient out-of-combat healing support
- Backup healing when the party wants to stretch resources
- Useful travel utility

**Best reminder:**
- This is usually more of a preparation/support spell than a combat spell

---

## Hide
**Type:** Core action use

**Cost:** Usually **Action**, unless another rule says otherwise

**What it does:**
- Lets you try to become unseen or unheard
- Usually uses your **Stealth +10**

**Why you use it:**
- Setting up ambushes
- Breaking enemy awareness
- Positioning before or during a fight

**Best reminder:**
- You are extremely good at Stealth, so this is one of your strongest non-spell tools

---

## Hunter's Lore
**Type:** Hunter feature

**Cost:** Passive trigger related to a **marked** target

**What it does:**
- Lets you learn a marked creature's **immunities, resistances, and vulnerabilities**

**Why you use it:**
- Helps the party stop wasting damage types
- Quickly tells you what a monster is resistant or vulnerable to

**Best reminder:**
- This is information value, not damage value
- Use it when the enemy seems strange, tough, or unusual

---

## Hunter's Mark
**Type:** Spell (Level 1)

**Cost:** **Bonus Action**, usually **1 spell slot or free cast**, **90 ft.**, **Concentration**

**What it does:**
- Marks one target
- Your weapon hits against that target deal extra damage
- It strongly supports repeated attacks against one enemy

**Why you use it:**
- It is one of your best damage tools in longer or important fights
- It gets better when you can keep attacking the same target over multiple rounds

**Best reminder:**
- Great against tougher enemies
- Not always worth it if a fight will end immediately
- Casting this ends **Fog Cloud**, **Pass without Trace**, or **Spike Growth** if you were concentrating on one of them

---

## Initiative

**Cost:** Passive

**What it does:**
- Determines how early you act in combat
- Your Initiative is **+7**

**Why you use it:**
- Going early lets you mark targets, reposition, or open from range before enemies act
- Works very well with your ranger playstyle

**Why you use it:**

**Cost:** Usually part of the **Attack** action

**What it does:**
- Attack bonus: **+9**
- Damage: **1d8 + 4 piercing**
- Best ranged weapon on your sheet

**Why you use it:**
- This is your default combat option
- It combines perfectly with **Archery**, **Extra Attack**, **Hunter's Mark**, and **Colossus Slayer**

## Longstrider
**Type:** Spell (Level 1)

**Cost:** **Action**, **1 spell slot**, **Touch**, no Concentration

**What it does:**
- Increases a creature's Speed by **10 ft.** for a long duration

**Why you use it:**
- Better positioning
- Easier kiting, chasing, scouting, or escape
- On yourself, it can push your speed from **40 ft. to 50 ft.**

**Best reminder:**
- Great before danger starts, because it does not need Concentration

---

**Cost:** **1 minute**, **Touch**, no spell slot

- Repairs a small break or tear in an object

**Why you use it:**
- Utility spell for fixing gear, containers, simple damage, or small broken objects

**Best reminder:**
- Not a combat spell

---

**Cost:** **Action**, **1 spell slot**, **Concentration**

# Quick “What should I do?” Guide

## If combat starts at range
- Use **Longbow**
- If the fight matters, cast **Hunter's Mark** first
- Then make **2 attacks** with **Extra Attack**

## If you expect stealth or infiltration
- Use **Stealth**
- Use **Pass without Trace** if the whole group needs help

## If the battlefield needs control
- Use **Spike Growth**
- Use **Fog Cloud** if vision denial matters more than damage

## If someone needs help
- **Cure Wounds** for direct healing
- **Goodberry** for low-pressure healing and support

## If you need movement
- Trust **Roving**
- Use **Longstrider** ahead of time when mobility matters

# One-line summary

**Floryn is a fast, stealthy ranged ranger whose default plan is to fight from position with a longbow, mark an important target, attack twice, and squeeze extra value from mobility, stealth, and wounded-target pressure.**
