# Floryn Supplemental Lookup Handbook

This document is a **supplement to your character sheet**, not a replacement for it.

## What stays on the sheet
Use your character sheet for:
- numbers
- attack bonuses
- damage dice
- AC / HP / speed
- spell slots
- prepared spells
- proficiencies
- inventory

## What lives in this document
Use this document for:
- **what an option is for**
- **what it costs to use**
- **what it actually does in play**
- **when it is usually worth using**
- **what conflicts with what**

---

# How to read the entries
Each entry uses the same fields:

- **Type** = attack, spell, feature, utility, or reminder
- **Cost** = action, bonus action, concentration, spell slot, passive, or time
- **Effect** = what happens
- **Use it when** = the practical reason to choose it
- **Remember** = the short tactical note that matters at the table

---

# Core play reminders

## Your default combat plan
Your normal plan is very simple:
1. Mark an important target with **Hunter's Mark**.
2. Use the **Attack** action.
3. Make two attacks because of **Extra Attack**.
4. Get **Colossus Slayer** value once per turn if the target is already wounded.

## Your main decision each fight
Your biggest choice is usually your **Concentration** spell.

Most of the time, that means choosing between:
- **Hunter's Mark** for single-target damage
- **Fog Cloud** for vision control and escape
- **Pass without Trace** for stealth and infiltration
- **Spike Growth** for area control and movement punishment

You can only keep **one** of those running at a time.

## What this character is best at
Floryn is strongest when he:
- fights at range
- starts from good position
- scouts ahead
- controls how enemies approach
- focuses one important target instead of spreading damage everywhere

# Lookup Entries (A-Z)

## Alert
**Type:** Feature  
**Cost:** Passive  
**Effect:** Helps you act early in initiative and gives you initiative-related utility from your background feature.  
**Use it when:** You want to set the pace of the fight before enemies can reposition.  
**Remember:** Going early is especially strong for you because early **Hunter's Mark**, early positioning, or early **Spike Growth** can shape the whole encounter.

## Attack Action
**Type:** Combat option  
**Cost:** 1 Action  
**Effect:** Lets you attack with the weapon shown on your sheet. Because you have **Extra Attack**, taking the Attack action gives you **two attacks** instead of one.  
**Use it when:** You want your normal, reliable turn.  
**Remember:** If you already have **Hunter's Mark** up and your target is wounded, this is usually your best damage turn.

## Clockwork Device
**Type:** Species utility  
**Cost:** 10 minutes, uses Prestidigitation  
**Effect:** You can make a Tiny clockwork device. You can have up to three at once, and each one lasts 8 hours.  
**Use it when:** You want a distraction, flavor tool, simple utility prop, or a clever non-combat setup.  
**Remember:** This is not raw combat power. It is a creativity tool.

## Colossus Slayer
**Type:** Subclass feature  
**Cost:** Passive  
**Effect:** Once on each of your turns, you deal extra damage to a target that is already wounded.  
**Use it when:** You are focusing a creature that has already taken any damage.  
**Remember:** It only cares that the target is already hurt. It does not need to be hurt by you.

## Concentration
**Type:** Spell reminder  
**Cost:** Ongoing focus  
**Effect:** While concentrating on one spell, you cannot keep another concentration spell running.  
**Use it when:** You are deciding whether damage, stealth, control, or escape matters most right now.  
**Remember:** Your main concentration conflict is usually **Hunter's Mark** vs **Pass without Trace** vs **Fog Cloud** vs **Spike Growth**.

## Cure Wounds
**Type:** Spell  
**Cost:** 1 Action, 1 spell slot, touch range  
**Effect:** Heals one creature you touch.  
**Use it when:** Someone is down, badly hurt, or you need direct healing right now.  
**Remember:** This costs your action and requires touch, so it is strongest as emergency healing or post-fight recovery, not as your default combat turn.  

## Deft Explorer
**Type:** Ranger feature  
**Cost:** Passive  
**Effect:** Gives you Expertise in one skill and two extra languages. On your sheet, this is the source of your Stealth Expertise and your extra languages.  
**Use it when:** You are scouting, sneaking, infiltrating, or dealing with creatures who speak your extra languages.  
**Remember:** This is one of the reasons Floryn feels more like a scout-hunter than a straight damage dealer.

## Extra Attack
**Type:** Ranger feature  
**Cost:** Passive, modifies the Attack action  
**Effect:** When you take the Attack action, you attack twice.  
**Use it when:** You want dependable damage every round.  
**Remember:** This is the backbone of your combat turns. Most of your offensive value comes from getting both attacks every round.

## Favored Enemy / Free Hunter's Mark Casts
**Type:** Ranger feature  
**Cost:** Passive resource  
**Effect:** **Hunter's Mark** is always prepared for you, and you get two free casts per long rest.  
**Use it when:** A fight matters enough that bonus damage on repeated hits will pay off.  
**Remember:** Spend the free casts first in important combats before burning spell slots.

## Fog Cloud
**Type:** Spell  
**Cost:** 1 Action, Concentration, 1 spell slot  
**Effect:** Creates a heavily obscured fog area that blocks clear sight through it.  
**Use it when:** You need to break line of sight, cover a retreat, disrupt enemy ranged attackers, or create confusion.  
**Remember:** This helps defense and escape more than damage. It also makes your own ranged attacks harder to use well if the fog gets in your way.

## Gnomish Cunning
**Type:** Species feature  
**Cost:** Passive  
**Effect:** Gives you strong protection against many mental saving throws.  
**Use it when:** You are facing charm, fear, illusions, mind effects, magical control, or other mental pressure.  
**Remember:** This is easy to forget because it is passive, but it is one of your strongest built-in defenses.

## Goodberry
**Type:** Spell  
**Cost:** 1 Action, 1 spell slot  
**Effect:** Creates ten magical berries that last for 24 hours. Each berry restores a small amount of health and provides a full day's nourishment.  
**Use it when:** You want efficient healing spread across the party, backup recovery prepared in advance, or an easy answer to food needs.  
**Remember:** This is usually a preparation spell, not a burst-combat spell.

## Hunter's Lore
**Type:** Subclass feature  
**Cost:** Passive rider on your marked target  
**Effect:** Lets you learn a marked creature's immunities, resistances, and vulnerabilities.  
**Use it when:** You are fighting something unfamiliar or tough enough that damage typing and monster knowledge matter.  
**Remember:** Marking a dangerous unknown enemy early can tell the party how to fight it smarter.

## Hunter's Mark
**Type:** Spell  
**Cost:** 1 Bonus Action, Concentration, free cast or 1 spell slot  
**Effect:** Marks one visible target, adds 1d6 extra damage when you hit it with attack rolls, and helps you track it.  
**Use it when:** You expect one target to stay alive for several hits.  
**Remember:** This is your standard boss / priority-target spell. It is weaker when enemies die too fast or when another concentration spell would swing the fight harder.

## Longstrider
**Type:** Spell  
**Cost:** 1 Action, 1 spell slot, no Concentration  
**Effect:** Increases a creature's speed for 1 hour.  
**Use it when:** You expect a chase, long approach, reposition-heavy fight, scouting mission, or need to kite enemies.  
**Remember:** Because you already move fast from **Roving**, this makes you especially hard to pin down.

## Mending
**Type:** Cantrip  
**Cost:** 1 minute  
**Effect:** Repairs a small break or tear in an object.  
**Use it when:** Gear, ropes, tools, straps, containers, or small objects get damaged.  
**Remember:** Slow, but useful. Think utility, not combat.

## Pass without Trace
**Type:** Spell  
**Cost:** 1 Action, Concentration, 1 spell slot  
**Effect:** Creates a stealth aura around you that boosts Stealth checks for you and chosen nearby creatures and helps hide your trail.  
**Use it when:** The whole group needs to sneak, infiltrate, avoid patrols, or leave fewer signs behind.  
**Remember:** This is one of your best team spells, but it competes directly with **Hunter's Mark** and **Spike Growth** for concentration.

## Prestidigitation
**Type:** Cantrip  
**Cost:** 1 Action  
**Effect:** Performs small magical tricks and cosmetic effects.  
**Use it when:** You want distraction, signaling, cleaning, flavor, or small utility effects.  
**Remember:** This is a creativity cantrip. Ask, improvise, and use it to support plans.

## Roving
**Type:** Ranger feature  
**Cost:** Passive  
**Effect:** Increases your movement speed and gives you climb and swim mobility while you are not in Heavy armor.  
**Use it when:** Terrain matters, enemies are trying to pin you down, or you want unusual approach angles.  
**Remember:** This is why Floryn is good at keeping distance, climbing for position, crossing water, and staying hard to trap.

## Spike Growth
**Type:** Spell  
**Cost:** 1 Action, Concentration, 1 spell slot  
**Effect:** Turns an area into difficult, damaging terrain. Creatures get punished for moving through it.  
**Use it when:** You want to block a path, protect a chokepoint, punish pursuit, split enemies, or force melee creatures to suffer for advancing.  
**Remember:** This is your best battlefield control spell. It is strongest when enemies must move.

## Stealth
**Type:** Skill reminder  
**Cost:** Usually no resource; sometimes your action or your plan  
**Effect:** Lets you scout, approach unseen, set ambushes, and avoid direct fights.  
**Use it when:** You can solve the problem better by getting position first instead of starting open combat.  
**Remember:** Stealth is one of your signature strengths because of **Deft Explorer**. **Pass without Trace** makes it even more powerful.

## Survival
**Type:** Skill reminder  
**Cost:** Usually no resource  
**Effect:** Helps with tracking, navigation, wilderness knowledge, and practical outdoors problem-solving.  
**Use it when:** The group needs to follow a trail, travel smart, read the land, or hunt something down.  
**Remember:** **Hunter's Mark** can also support the tracking side of your kit.

## Thieves' Tools
**Type:** Utility proficiency  
**Cost:** Usually an action or more, depending on the situation  
**Effect:** Lets you handle locks and other tool-based criminal utility.  
**Use it when:** A direct approach is loud, risky, or unnecessary.  
**Remember:** This is part of Floryn's old criminal skill set and still one of his best non-combat problem-solving tools.
